Our Mission

Empowering the next generation of voters

NextGenVotes is a nonpartisan youth civic engagement organization dedicated to helping young people become informed and active members of their communities. We believe every student should have the knowledge and confidence to understand how government works, how elections affect their lives, and how they can make a difference. By expanding student led chapters across New Jersey, we are working to make practical, nonpartisan civic education accessible to more students while empowering them to educate others, strengthen their communities, and inspire lifelong civic participation.

Voting 101

Your guide to casting a ballot

New to voting? This is your starting point: clear, nonpartisan basics on registering, choosing how to vote, and showing up with confidence.

Why your vote matters

Voting is how you shape the issues that affect your school, your community, and your future, from local budgets to national policy. Every election counts, not just presidential years. Your voice belongs in every race on the ballot. No party or candidate gets our endorsement. We share just the facts you need to participate.

Three ways to vote in New Jersey

Election Day

Vote in person at your assigned polling place on Election Day. Polls in New Jersey are typically open from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

Find your polling location before you go. It may not be your school.

Find your polling place

Early Voting

New Jersey offers in person early voting at designated locations in the days leading up to Election Day. No excuse needed.

Early voting locations differ from your Election Day polling place.

View early voting locations

Vote by Mail

Request a mail in ballot and return it by mail or at a secure drop box. A great option if you'll be away or prefer voting from home.

Apply early and mail your ballot back well before the deadline.

Apply for a mail in ballot
